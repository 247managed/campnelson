Dedication

To Those Who Gave Everything

To every soldier who stood in the gap between freedom and its enemies,

To every man and woman who laid aside the comforts of home and walked into the long uncertain dark of war,

To the United States Colored Troops who mustered at Camp Nelson, who came in chains and left as soldiers, who proved by their blood and by their valor that freedom is not given — it is purchased,

To those who rest in Kentucky's earth at this hallowed ground, known and unknown, remembered and forgotten by name but not forgotten by God,

These songs are offered.

May they rise like incense from this green and storied ground.

May they carry your names into the hearing of every generation.

May they remind us, the living, that we are caretakers of a freedom we did not earn.

“Greater love hath no man than this, that a man lay down his life for his friends.”

— John 15:13
