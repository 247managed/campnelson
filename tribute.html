To Michael,

There are people in this world whose work is simply a job, and then there are people whose work is a calling. You, Michael, are one of the called.

As Director of Camp Nelson National Cemetery, you have been entrusted with something far greater than grounds and granite. You have been entrusted with the legacy of thousands of American heroes — men and women who gave the last full measure of devotion so that this nation might endure. And you have honored that trust with a steadfastness, dignity, and tenderness that words can scarcely capture.

Every morning you walk among the headstones, and you do not see rows of marble. You see people. You see fathers, mothers, sons, daughters, husbands, and wives. You see the young private who never made it home and the old veteran who served a full career and asked only for a quiet resting place at the end. You see them all, and you care for them all.

Guardian of Honor

You ensure that every headstone stands straight, every flag flies with purpose, and every family who visits finds a place of peace and reverence worthy of their loved one's sacrifice.

Shepherd of Families

You greet the grieving with compassion that cannot be taught. You stand with families in their darkest hours, offering not just professionalism but genuine human warmth and grace.

Keeper of Legacy

Through your devotion, the stories of those who rest at Camp Nelson National Cemetery will never be forgotten. You are the living bridge between their sacrifice and our memory.

The grounds of Camp Nelson National Cemetery are more than a cemetery under your care — they are a sanctuary. You have made this hallowed earth a place where history breathes, where sacrifice is felt in the stillness of the morning air, and where every visitor leaves changed.

You carry a weight that most people will never understand: the weight of being the last person to ensure that a veteran's final resting place reflects the honor of their service. And you carry it not with burden, but with purpose. Not with weariness, but with love.

These hymns were written because of places like Camp Nelson National Cemetery and people like you. Your dedication inspired the words. Your faithfulness gave them meaning. Your service to the fallen is itself a kind of hymn — sung not with music, but with action, every single day.

“

A cemetery director does not merely maintain the grounds where heroes sleep. He stands as the final sentinel — the last one who will ensure that their sacrifice is treated with the honor it deserves, long after the world has moved on.

Michael, this hymnal is dedicated to the veterans of Camp Nelson National Cemetery, but it is also dedicated to you — because without people like you, the memory of their service would fade into silence. You are the reason the silence speaks.

Thank you for your service to those who served. Thank you for your faithfulness. Thank you for being the faithful servant that Camp Nelson National Cemetery — and this nation — so deeply needs.

With deepest respect and gratitude,

The Author of These Hymns
