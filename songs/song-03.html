Hymn 03

Brother's Keeper

The Hymn
0:00
The Prayer
0:00

Verse 1 Am I my brother's keeper?
The ancient question cried,
And from across the ages,
The soldier's answer: I tried.

Chorus I am my brother's keeper,
I'll stand beside his name,
I'll carry him through fire,
Through darkness, wind, and rain.
I am my brother's keeper,
Through every war and strife,
The greatest gift a man can give,
Is laying down his life.

Verse 2 He did not ask for glory,
He did not seek the fame,
He only knew the duty,
That called him to this plain.

Verse 3 So let us be their keepers now,
And keep their memory near,
And tell the story proudly,
Of all they held most dear.

Bridge Greater love hath no man,
Than to lay his life down,
For the stranger, for the brother,
For the nation, for the crown.
