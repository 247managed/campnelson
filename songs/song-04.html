Hymn 04

Freedom's River

The Hymn
0:00
The Prayer
0:00

Verse 1 Down beside the Kentucky River,
Where the tall sycamores stand,
Men in blue came marching forward,
Reaching out a trembling hand.
Black and white together standing,
On the banks of Nelson's ground,
In the cause of one great freedom,
Finally to be found.

Chorus Freedom's river keeps on flowing,
Down through history's long years,
Bearing all the tears of anguish,
Bearing all the victor's cheers.
Let it flow through every valley,
Let it reach from shore to shore,
For the ones who crossed this river,
Let us honor them once more.

Verse 2 Men who never knew a schoolroom,
Men who never knew their worth,
Took the musket, took the blue coat,
Proved their freedom's right on Earth.
They came hungry, they came barefoot,
To this camp beside the stream,
They laid down their chains in earnest,
And they rose to chase a dream.

Verse 3 Now the river still runs onward,
And the memory rolls along,
Of the men who bought our freedom,
With their sacrifice and song.
