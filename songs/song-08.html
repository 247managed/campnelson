Hymn 08

Home on Nelson's Ground

The Hymn
0:00
The Prayer
0:00

Verse 1 Where the rolling hills of Kentucky,
Meet the river winding down,
There's a place of peace and memory,
There's a hallowed, holy ground.
Men from North and men from South met,
Men in freedom, men in chains,
And together in God's service,
Gave their lives upon these plains.

Chorus Home on Nelson's ground, dear soldier,
Home where memory and morning meet,
Home where God has marked your resting,
Home beneath the clover's feet.
Rest on Nelson's ground, dear soldier,
All your marching now is done,
Kentucky's hills will be your pillow,
Sleep beneath the setting sun.

Verse 2 In the summer, roses scatter,
On the stones where brave men lie,
And the children come to wonder,
At the names they're passing by.
"Who were these?" the children question,
And we answer, "They were men,
Who gave everything they carried,
So that peace might come again."

Verse 3 When the Judgment Day is calling,
And the graves all open wide,
From this ground in Nelson's valley,
They will walk with God's own pride.
And the nation they had purchased,
With the currency of pain,
Will stand before its Maker,
And say: "Lord, it was not in vain."
