Hymn 11

Lay Down the Sword

The Hymn
0:00
The Prayer
0:00

Verse 1 There will come a day, dear soldier,
When the war no more shall be,
When the cannon finds its silence,
And the prisoner is free.
When the widow stops her weeping,
When the orphan finds a home,
When the nations all stop fighting,
And no soldier walks alone.

Chorus Lay down the sword in God's own mercy,
Lay down the rifle and the gun,
All the fighting now is finished,
All the marching has been done.
Lay down your burden, weary soldier,
Lay it at the Master's feet,
In the courts of Heaven's glory,
You have earned your honored seat.

Verse 2 We who live in peace behind you,
Breathe the air you died to save,
And we walk on ground more holy,
For the sacrifice you gave.
Every freedom that we cherish,
Every right we freely own,
Was a flower bought with bloodshed,
Was a seed that someone sown.

Verse 3 In the land beyond the river,
In the land where shadows cease,
You will finally meet your Maker,
You will finally find your peace.
He will take the sword from off you,
He will take the worn cartridge case,
He will give you rest and garlands,
He will let you see His face.
