Hymn 11

Lay Down the Sword

The Hymn
0:00
The Prayer
0:00

Verse 1 There will come a day, dear soldier,
When the war no more shall be,
When the cannon finds its silence,
And the prisoner is free.
When the widow stops her weeping,
When the orphan finds a home,
When the nations all stop fighting,
And no soldier walks alone.

Chorus Lay down the sword in God's own mercy,
Lay down the rifle and the gun,
All the fighting now is finished,
All the marching has been done.
Lay down your burden, weary soldier,
Lay it at the Master's feet,
In the courts of Heaven's glory,
You have earned your honored seat.

Verse 2 We who live in peace behind you,
Breathe the air you died to save,
And we walk on ground more holy,
For the sacrifice you gave.
Every freedom that we cherish,
Every right we freely own,
Was a flower bought with bloodshed,
Was a seed that someone sown.

Verse 3 In the land beyond the river,
In the land where shadows cease,
You will finally meet your Maker,
You will finally find your peace.
He will take the sword from off you,
He will take the worn cartridge case,
He will give you rest and garlands,
He will let you see His face.

A Prayer of Final Peace — The Laying Down

Lord God, who holds the last day in Your hands,

We confess that we live in a world that does not yet look like the one described in this song. The cannon has not found its silence. The prisoner is not yet free. The widow is still weeping in houses on streets in countries we could name, and the orphan has not yet found his home, and the nations have not stopped their fighting, and soldiers are walking alone tonight in places the evening news has already forgotten. We do not come to this ground pretending otherwise. We come with our eyes open and our hands empty, standing between what is and what You have promised, and asking You to hold us steady in that in-between place.

But we also come, Father, with a hope that is not wishful thinking but revealed promise — Your own word spoken over the arc of history, bending it toward a day when the sword is laid down for the last time, not in defeat but in completion. When the fighting is not merely paused but finished. When the march is not interrupted but done. We believe that day is coming. We believe it with the specific, stubborn faith of those who have stood at graves like these and refused to accept that the last word belongs to the grave. And we ask You today to let that coming day cast its light backward over this ground — over these names, these stones, these sleeping soldiers — and illuminate what they were part of: the long, costly, faithful human march toward the peace that only You can finally give.

We breathe the air they died to save. We know that most of the time we breathe it without thinking — without the slightest awareness of the price encoded in each breath. Forgive us for that, Lord. And on the days when we do remember, when the weight of it lands on us suddenly and unexpectedly the way grief sometimes does — let the remembering not crush us but commission us. Let it make us worthy of the inheritance. Let every freedom we cherish carry, somewhere in its keeping, the face of someone who paid for it with the only currency they had.

We speak now of the thing this song describes with such tender precision — the moment of arrival. The land beyond the river, where shadows cease. The moment the weary soldier stands before his Maker and the sword is taken from him, not seized but received — gently, the way a father takes a heavy thing from a child who has carried it far enough. The worn cartridge case, the burden of years, the weight of what was witnessed and what was done and what was endured — all of it lifted. All of it received. And in its place: rest. Garlands. The face of God.

That is not a small thing to imagine. It is almost too large to hold. But we hold it today, Lord — we hold it deliberately and with both hands — for every soldier in this ground who carried their burden all the way to the end and laid it finally at Your feet. Let them have received everything this song promises. Let the honored seat in Heaven's courts be theirs. Let the face they sought through all the smoke and shadow be the face they are now seeing, clearly and without obstruction, in the light that has no evening.

Lay it down now, soldier. The rifle. The cartridge case. The long weight of all you saw and could not unsee. The years of marching. The nights of watching. The friends you could not bring home. Lay it all at His feet — He has been waiting to take it since before you ever picked it up. And in its place He gives you His face. And that is enough. That is more than enough. That is everything.

Until that day when all wars are ended and every soldier is finally, fully home — we stand here, we remember, we speak the names, and we hold the promise. In the name of the Prince of Peace, who is making all things new, we pray.

Amen.
