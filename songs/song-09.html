Hymn 09

Though the Body Sleeps

The Hymn
0:00
The Prayer
0:00

Verse 1 Though the body sleeps in silence,
Underneath the winter ground,
Though the name is worn by weather,
On a stone no longer sound,
We believe in resurrection,
We believe in life above,
For the God who made the soldier,
Is a God of endless love.

Chorus Though the body sleeps, the spirit,
Walks in light beyond our sight,
Every fallen one has risen,
Into God's eternal light.
We will meet them in the morning,
When this mortal dream has ceased,
And the last great war is over,
And the dead have been released.

Verse 2 Do not mourn without a reason,
Do not weep without a hope,
For the ones who rest in Nelson,
Have been given more than scope.
They have walked through death's own valley,
And have found it not so deep,
For the Shepherd walks beside them,
Even as they fall to sleep.

Verse 3 So we lay the wreath in season,
And we fold the flag with care,
Not in hopeless, hollow sorrow,
But in faith beyond compare.
We believe the dead shall answer,
When the Trumpet calls their name,
And the soldier long forgotten,
Will at last receive his fame.

Bridge Sleep now, soldier, in God's mercy,
You have earned your long repose,
We will keep your light from fading,
As this earthly story closes.
