Hymn 12

By Kentucky Waters

The Hymn
0:00
The Prayer
0:00

Verse 1 By these Kentucky waters,
The men in blue once stood,
And some had walked through bondage,
And some through field and wood.
They came from every quarter,
They came from far and near,
But all were bound by one thing:
The promise ringing clear.

Chorus By Kentucky waters,
They were washed and made new,
Free and bound and broken,
In the gray and in the blue.
By Kentucky waters,
Where the old camp met the field,
Something more than armies,
Was broken and was healed.

Verse 2 The preachers prayed at Nelson,
The chaplains read the Word,
And even in the darkness,
A soldier's prayer was heard.
By the creekside near the camp ground,
Beneath the sycamore's shade,
Men knelt before their Maker,
Unafraid yet still afraid.

Verse 3 Now we come to these same waters,
Now we stand on this same shore,
And we feel the weight of history,
Pressing down from days of yore.
Lord, remind us of our calling,
As You called those men to give,
May we serve our brother freely,
May we help our neighbor live.

Bridge Let the river keep on running,
Let the story keep its flow,
Let the water tell the story,
Of the seeds that brave men sow.
