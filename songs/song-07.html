Hymn 07

The Watch They Kept

The Hymn
0:00
The Prayer
0:00

Verse 1 Through the night they kept their watching,
Through the frost and through the rain,
Through the fear and through the longing,
For the warmth of home again.
Yet they did not leave their post, Lord,
Though the darkness felt so deep,
For they knew the ones behind them,
Were the ones they had to keep.

Chorus The watch they kept was not for nothing,
The post they held was not in vain,
For the peace we breathe this morning,
Was the price of all their pain.
Honor, honor to the faithful,
Who stood firm through wind and cold,
May we keep a watch as steady,
May their courage make us bold.

Verse 2 In the hospitals at Nelson,
In the tents beside the road,
Women knelt to serve the wounded,
Helping soldiers bear their load.
They were also in the battle,
Armed with bandage, lamp, and prayer,
In the dark of death and sickness,
Still they kept their faithful care.

Verse 3 Now the watch has been completed,
And the weary ones may rest,
They have stood their final duty,
They have met their final test.
God of Heaven, keep their memory,
As we pass this ground in awe,
For the watch that they were given,
Was the highest watch of all.

A Prayer for the Faithful Watch

God of the long night and the breaking dawn,

We remember today those who stood when standing cost everything — who held their post through frost and darkness and longing, when every instinct in them cried out for warmth and rest and home. They did not leave. That is the whole of it, and it is enough to break the heart with gratitude. The ones behind them were sleeping, or healing, or simply living — and that ordinary life was made possible by the ones on the line who refused to let the darkness through. They gave us the morning we woke into today. They have been giving it to us ever since.

But we come today also to name those whose watch was kept without a weapon in hand — the women who moved through the hospital tents at Camp Nelson National Cemetery with bandage and lamp and prayer, kneeling in the dark beside men who were afraid, holding the hands of the dying, speaking words of comfort into ears that may not have heard anything after. They were also in the battle. Let us say that plainly and with force, Father, because history has not always said it and it must be said: they were also in the battle. The courage required to walk into a ward full of suffering and disease and despair and stay — to keep returning day after day, night after night — was not a lesser courage. It was simply a different one, and it was indispensable, and without it fewer men would have lived to keep the watch another day.

Lord, we confess how little we know of the weight of a long watch. We live in the peace that their vigilance purchased and rarely feel the cost of it. The frost they stood in has long since melted. The darkness they held back has given way to the ordinary brightness of our days. We move through those days without thinking, most of the time, of what it took to make them ordinary. Forgive our forgetting. And restore in us, today, standing on this ground, the full and sober awareness of what it cost.

You are a God who keeps watch, Lord. You neither slumber nor sleep — the Psalmist told us so, and these men and women may have clung to that truth in the coldest hours of their longest nights. We ask that You receive their faithfulness as the offering it was. Not a sacrifice made in bitterness or under compulsion, but the willing, clear-eyed gift of those who knew the ones behind them were worth every hour of the keeping.

The watch has been completed now. The final duty is met. The weary ones have laid their post down and stepped at last into the rest they could not take while we still needed them. May they find, in that rest, everything the long night kept them from — the warmth, the welcome, the voice of the One who was watching over them the whole time they were watching over us.

Honor to the soldier in the frost. Honor to the woman with the lamp. Honor to the ones whose names we know and the ones whose names the wind took. They stood their watch. They did not leave their post. And the morning we are standing in is the morning they made possible with the only currency they had — their faithful, unrepeatable lives.

Make us bold, Lord, with the courage we have inherited from them. Set a watch in our own generation that does not falter. And keep their memory alive in this ground and in our hearts until the highest watch of all is ended and You Yourself call every faithful sentinel home.

With honor, with reverence, and with lasting gratitude, we pray.

Amen.
