Hymn 14

Marching to a Better Shore

The Hymn
0:00
The Prayer
0:00

Verse 1 Oh, they marched through rain and sunshine,
Oh, they marched through mud and snow,
From the mountains to the lowlands,
Wherever duty bid them go.
But the march that counts in glory,
Is the march that they march now,
Through the golden gates of Heaven,
With the sweat still on their brow.

Chorus Marching to a better shore, oh yes,
Marching where there is no war,
All the weary, all the wounded,
Find the peace they're marching for.
Marching in the light of glory,
Marching free forevermore,
Oh, the soldier has enlisted,
In the Lord's own glorious corps.

Verse 2 Do you hear the drum of Heaven,
Do you hear the angel's fife,
Calling all the faithful soldiers,
Onward into endless life?
They are done with earthly armor,
They are done with earthly pain,
Now they wear the robe of victory,
And they'll never march in rain.

Verse 3 When I come to cross that river,
When I lay this body down,
Let me march with all the soldiers,
Who have earned the victor's crown.
Let me march beside the farmer,
Who became a captain proud,
Let me march beside the freed man,
Who is marching tall and loud.

Bridge Hallelujah! March, ye faithful!
Hallelujah! March on home!
All the battles have been finished,
And no soldier walks alone!
