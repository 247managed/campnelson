Upon This Sacred Ground

The Hymn
0:00

0:00

Verse 1 Beneath these stones the faithful lie,
Who gave their lives beneath God's sky,
No greater gift hath mortal known,
Than seeds of freedom, blood-red, sown.

Chorus Upon this sacred ground we stand,
Redeemed by sacrifice and hand,
O God, receive the ones who gave,
Their lives beyond this earthly grave.

Verse 2 From Kentucky's hills they came to serve,
With steadfast heart and steady nerve,
They left their homes, their kin, their name,
And bore this nation's grief and shame.

Chorus Upon this sacred ground we stand,
Redeemed by sacrifice and hand,
O God, receive the ones who gave,
Their lives beyond this earthly grave.

Verse 3 The bugle calls no more to arms,
The cannon still, no more alarms,
Yet still their valor fills the air,
A holy hush — God's answered prayer.

Bridge We will not let their memory fade,
We'll honor every choice they made,
And tell our children, every one,
What love is done beneath God's sun.

Final Chorus Upon this sacred ground we kneel,
Before the wounds that time can't heal,
O God, receive the ones who gave,
Their lives beyond this earthly grave.

A Prayer for the Families of the Fallen

Gracious and merciful God,

We gather on this sacred ground, beneath Your endless sky, with hearts both heavy and full — full of gratitude for what has been given, and heavy with the weight of what was laid down and never returned.

We speak now, Lord, not only of the ones whose names are carved in stone — but to those who carry those names within their hearts. To the mothers who kept a candle burning long after the house grew quiet. To the fathers who answered every question with steady dignity they had to borrow from somewhere deep. To the children who grew up with a photograph instead of a hand to hold. To the husbands and wives who learned the terrible grammar of grief — how to speak of a life in the past tense, and live forward in it still.

Lord, let these families know — let them feel it in the very marrow of their bones — that the sacrifice they bore was not a private one. It was gathered up by this nation and held as sacred. They, too, served. They, too, gave. When a soldier walked through the door for the last time, a family followed them into the breach — not in uniform, but in love. They gave not only their beloved. They gave a part of themselves that cannot be reclaimed.

Console the grief that does not announce itself, Lord — the grief that wakes in the early hours, that sits quietly at the holiday table, that lingers in old photographs and familiar hymns. Comfort those who have carried this weight for decades, and those who are only now learning how heavy it truly is.

We thank You, Father, for the character You shaped in these men and women — for the courage and conviction, the faithfulness and love of country and kin that led them to stand in the gap so that others would not have to. Let their memory be not only a wound, but a light. Let their children and their children's children look upon how they lived and be made braver, kinder, and more worthy of the freedom bought with such a price.

And we ask, Lord — receive them. Receive those who gave all. Bring peace to the hearts of those who still search for it this side of eternity. Let this sacred ground be not only a place of mourning, but of meeting — where the living commune with the honored dead, and where grief is gathered up and held within Your grace.

We will not forget them. We will not let their families stand alone. And until the bugle sounds one final time and all accounts are settled by Your hand — we will tend this ground, speak these names, and carry forward the love they left behind.

In Your name, and in their honor, we pray.

Amen.
