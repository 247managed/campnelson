Hymn 15

Rise, Faithful Soldier

The Hymn
0:00
The Prayer
0:00

Verse 1 Rise, faithful soldier, from your sleeping,
Rise and hear your nation call,
Not the call of drum to battle,
But the call to stand up tall.
Rise and let your story echo,
Through the centuries yet to be,
Rise and know your life has purchased,
Something more than victory.

Chorus Rise, faithful soldier, God has seen you,
Rise, your name is not forgot,
Rise from every field of sorrow,
Rise from every hallowed spot.
We are standing here to tell you,
What your sacrifice has done,
You have kept the torch of freedom,
Burning underneath the sun.

Verse 2 You were young once, you were fearful,
You were flesh and bone and breath,
But you chose a greater calling,
And you walked it unto death.
And in choosing for your neighbor,
In the choosing for the least,
You have made yourself immortal,
From the Western to the East.

Verse 3 So we dedicate this book of songs,
To you who rest below,
Every hymn a stone of memory,
Every verse a torch's glow.
Camp Nelson National Cemetery holds your body gently,
In Kentucky's dark brown earth,
And until the Resurrection,
We will guard your sacred worth.

Final Verse Rise, faithful soldier, God has seen you,
Rise from blue and gray and black,
Every color, every soldier,
We have come to bring you back,
Into the light of true remembrance,
Into the hearts of those who live,
No monument of stone or music,
Can repay the gift you give.

A Prayer of Dedication — The Closing of the Book

Lord God, Keeper of every name, Witness of every sacrifice,

We have come to the end of the book. Not the end of the story — never that — but the end of our particular telling of it, this collection of songs and prayers and remembrances that began in grief and moved through brotherhood and sacrifice and the waters of Camp Nelson National Cemetery and the march through glory and has arrived here, at last, at the song that gathers all the others up and offers them back to the ones they were written for. We lay this book down now at Your feet, and at theirs, and we ask that You receive it as what it was always meant to be: not a monument, not a performance, not a product — but a stone of memory, a torch's glow, an act of love directed toward the dead who cannot read it and the living who desperately need to.

We speak now — one final time, in this prayer, over this collection — directly to the ones who rest at Camp Nelson National Cemetery in Kentucky's dark brown earth. You were young once. You were fearful. You were flesh and bone and breath exactly like the people standing over your graves today, exactly like the children tracing your letters, exactly like the families who still feel the shape of the space you left behind. You were not made of different material than the rest of us. You were made of the same uncertain, breakable, mortal stuff. And you chose anyway. You chose the greater calling. You chose your neighbor. You chose the least. And in that choosing — in that one irrevocable, costly, beautiful act of choosing — you stepped out of the ordinary stream of days and into something that has not stopped rippling since.

We have tried, Lord — in fifteen songs and fifteen prayers and all the imperfect, reaching, insufficient words that human beings can muster — to bring them back. Into the light of true remembrance. Into the hearts of the living where they belong. We know that no monument of stone can repay what they gave. We know that no music, however faithful, closes the distance between what was given and what can be returned. But we tried. And we ask You to take the trying and make it more than it is — to breathe into these songs what only You can breathe, so that when a child hears them, or a widow, or a veteran who knows what the mud actually costs, something moves in them that no song alone could move. Let Your Spirit carry what our words cannot.

Every color, every soldier — blue and gray and black, from the mountains to the lowlands, from Kentucky's hills to the fields where they fell and the fields they never left and the fields they came home from changed forever. Every rank and every date on every stone that is still sound and every stone that has worn past reading. Every freed man who rose from chains to claim a uniform. Every farm boy who became a captain and did not live to come home. Every woman in the hospital tent who kept her faithful, lamp-lit watch. Every chaplain who read the Word over the dying. Every family who received the news and had to go on living. This book is for all of them. This prayer is for all of them. And the One to whom we pray holds all of them — has always held all of them — in a remembrance that makes ours look like a candle beside the sun.

Camp Nelson National Cemetery: may this ground continue to be what it has always been — not merely a resting place, but a teaching place. A place where the living come to learn what they could not learn anywhere else, in the particular grammar of stone and silence and the Kentucky earth that holds these men so gently. Guard them well, Lord. Guard their sacred worth until the morning when guarding is no longer needed because the Resurrection has done what no cemetery can — given back what death presumed to take and proved, once and finally, that You have never once let go.

Rise, faithful soldier
from the blue and the gray and the black,
from the frost and the mud and the rain,
from the hospital tent and the creekside prayer,
from the letter folded and sealed
and the name worn smooth on the stone,
from the long and faithful watch,
from the field beside the Kentucky river,
from the dark brown earth that holds you now
so gently, so faithfully,
until the morning.

God has seen you.
Your name is not forgot.
We are standing here to tell you —
we have always been standing here —
and we will not stop
until the torch you gave us
is passed to every hand
that needs its light
and the story
is fully
told.

We dedicate this book, these songs, these prayers, these stones of memory and torches of verse, to the faithful soldiers of Camp Nelson National Cemetery, and to every person who loved them, and to every generation that will come to this ground and ask the question that must never stop being asked: Who were these?

In the name of the God who sees, who remembers, who is coming to raise every faithful soldier from every hallowed spot — we close this book, we lift this prayer.

Amen.
