Hymn 05

Carry the Colors Home

The Hymn
0:00
The Prayer
0:00

Verse 1 When the march is over, soldier,
When the battle smoke has cleared,
And the trumpet sounds the final,
And the last enemy feared,
You will lay your colors gently,
At the feet of God's own throne,
And the angels all will witness,
As you carry the colors home.

Chorus Carry the colors home, soldier,
Carry them high and proud,
You have run the race with honor,
You have done what God allowed.
From the fields of war's long shadow,
Through the valley, on toward the dome,
Lay your burden down in glory,
Carry the colors home.

Verse 2 We who walk the earth behind you,
Cannot know what price you paid,
We can only lift your standard,
And stand firm without afraid.
For the colors that you carried,
Through the smoke and through the foam,
Are the colors of a nation,
That you carried safely home.

Bridge Stars and stripes and something holy,
In the hands of those who gave,
Every stripe a mark of mercy,
Every star above a grave.
