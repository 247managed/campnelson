Hymn 05

Carry the Colors Home

The Hymn
0:00
The Prayer
0:00

Verse 1 When the march is over, soldier,
When the battle smoke has cleared,
And the trumpet sounds the final,
And the last enemy feared,
You will lay your colors gently,
At the feet of God's own throne,
And the angels all will witness,
As you carry the colors home.

Chorus Carry the colors home, soldier,
Carry them high and proud,
You have run the race with honor,
You have done what God allowed.
From the fields of war's long shadow,
Through the valley, on toward the dome,
Lay your burden down in glory,
Carry the colors home.

Verse 2 We who walk the earth behind you,
Cannot know what price you paid,
We can only lift your standard,
And stand firm without afraid.
For the colors that you carried,
Through the smoke and through the foam,
Are the colors of a nation,
That you carried safely home.

Bridge Stars and stripes and something holy,
In the hands of those who gave,
Every stripe a mark of mercy,
Every star above a grave.

A Prayer at the Final Muster

Lord God of every army, Captain of every soul,

There is a moment we cannot see from here — a moment beyond the smoke and the silence, beyond the folded flag and the rifle volley and the long drive home — where a soldier stands before Your throne and lays down what he carried. Not in defeat. Not in exhaustion, though the road was long. But in the completion of a mission faithfully run, a race honestly finished, a charge kept all the way to the end. We believe that moment is real. We believe You were watching for them before they could see You. And we gather here today around that belief, and around the ones who loved this soldier, and we ask You to make it enough.

We do not pretend to know what they endured. The smoke was not ours to breathe. The weight was not ours to carry. The fear in the long dark hours before the dawn — we can read about it, we can imagine the edges of it, but the center of it belongs to them alone, and to You who walked with them through it. What we know is this: they bore it. They did not set it down. They carried the colors through everything the world threw at them, and they carried them high, and they brought them home.

We ask You now, Father, to speak to the hearts of those who must receive that folded flag with trembling hands. To the wife who is learning what it means to be brave in a way she never asked to learn. To the children who do not fully understand yet, but who feel in their bones that something immense has shifted in the world. To the parents who held this soldier as an infant and cannot reconcile that beginning with this end. Surround them, Lord. Be closer to them in this hour than their nearest friend. Let them feel — not as sentiment, but as substance — that the One who watched over their beloved in every theater of war is watching over them now in this.

We look at the flag today, Lord, and we see it differently than we did before. We see it the way this song teaches us to see it — not as cloth and dye and careful geometry, but as something consecrated. Every stripe a mark of mercy, given by hands that could have held back and did not. Every star a candle over a grave, burning in honor of a name. Let us never again lift or receive or fold this flag without feeling the full and holy weight of what it actually is.

And for those of us who walk the earth behind them — grant us the grace not to waste what we were given. The colors they carried were not only fabric. They were promise. They were the embodied oath that this nation, for all its failures and fractures, is worth the cost of keeping. They carried that promise through the valley and through the shadow and delivered it still intact into our hands. Let us be worthy stewards. Let us stand firm. Let us lift the standard they set down and carry it forward in our own generation with at least a fraction of the courage it cost them.

The march is over for this soldier. The battle smoke has cleared. The last enemy has been faced and the final trumpet has sounded, and we trust — we hold with both hands to the trust — that the angels were witnesses, and that the welcome was everything the faithfulness deserved.

Rest now, soldier. The colors are home. The burden is laid down. The race is run with honor. And the God who gave you the mission was the first one waiting at the finish line.

With gratitude beyond our words and hope beyond our grief, we pray.

Amen.
